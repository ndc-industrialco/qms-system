# Notification Rules

## Purpose

Define when and how the system should notify users or operators.

## Rules

- Choose the right channel for the message: user-facing, email, or operational alert.
- User-facing notifications must be clear, concise, and actionable.
- Operational alerts must include enough context to investigate.
- Do not use notifications as a substitute for audit logs.
- Avoid duplicate notifications unless the workflow intentionally requires them.
- Define retry, fallback, or fail-open behavior for notification delivery.
- Keep notification content aligned with the business action that triggered it.
- If a notification is generated by a user action, include the actor and resource context.

## Minimum Expectations

- channel choice
- trigger context
- actionability
- failure behavior

## Failure Conditions

- Notifications are duplicated, silent, or sent to the wrong channel without a defined behavior.
- A notification lacks the context needed to understand why it was sent.
- A notification is used where audit or state tracking should have been used instead.
